\practiceSheetTitle{\textcircled{\scriptsize\normalfont\sffamily S} \gettext{Plan out your practice routine}}
	\textbf{\gettext{How many times per week I listen to an mindfulness exercise:}} \Blank{1}

	\gettext{We recommend listening to a mindfulness exercise on a daily or semi-daily basis (4 to 6 days per week). Regular practice will keep your “mindfulness muscle” strong over time.}

	\textbf{\gettext{The time of day I usually will practice is:}}\Blank{2}

	\gettext{Here are some example times of day to practice that work for other people:}
	\begin{itemize*}\gettext{
		\item In the morning: before breakfast, after showering, when getting ready
		\item At night: after putting on pajamas, during nighttime unwind routine
		\item Midday: during lunch break
	}\end{itemize*}

	\textbf{\gettext{How am I going to access the audio-guided exercises? [?]}} \Blank{2}

	\textbf{\gettext{I will practice in the following location:}} \Blank{1}

	\gettext{Here are some potential options: (1) Bedroom, (2) On the bus (with headphones), (3) Car (while parked), (4) Office, (5) Outside.}

	\textbf{\gettext{I will practice in the following position/posture:}} \Blank{2}

	\gettext{We recommend sitting upright, rather than slouching. For the options below, use pillows as needed to support your back.}
	\begin{enumerate*}\gettext{
		\item Sit in a chair with feet on floor
		\item Sit on couch in a cross-legged position or with feet on floor
		\item Sit on a bed in a cross-legged position
		\item Sit cross-legged on the floor (maybe with back against wall)
	}\end{enumerate*}
	\gettext{Lying down is an option too. However, it can be easier to get sleepy in this position. If you get too sleepy lying down, then try sitting upright in one of the positions listed above.}
